Meet the Band

Harvey Dale

Harvey Dale is the soulful guitarist and songwriter of "Harvey Dale and the Cements," an acclaimed indie rock band hailing from Nashville, Tennessee. Born and raised in Music City, Harvey's musical journey began at the age of 10 when he picked up his father's old acoustic guitar. His passionate and emotive guitar solos have become the signature sound of the band, earning him a dedicated fan following. Harvey's lyrics often draw from personal experiences, reflecting his introspective nature. When he's not on stage or in the studio, Harvey can be found exploring the rich music scene of Nashville, seeking inspiration from the city's vibrant culture.

Sally Sue

Sally Sue is the dynamic lead vocalist and bassist of "Harvey Dale and the Cements." Originally from the bustling streets of Brooklyn, New York, Sally's powerful and hauntingly beautiful voice has been captivating audiences since she was a teenager. With a background in theater, she brings a theatrical flair to the band's live performances, creating an electrifying stage presence. Sally's lyrics are deeply introspective and often explore themes of self-discovery and empowerment. When she's not on tour, Sally enjoys visiting art galleries, collecting vintage records, and honing her skills as a budding painter.

Roger Williams

Roger Williams, the rhythmic heartbeat of "Harvey Dale and the Cements," is the band's drummer and percussionist. Born and raised in the musical melting pot of New Orleans, Roger's infectious energy and diverse percussion skills infuse the band's sound with a unique groove. He's known for his eclectic taste in music, drawing inspiration from jazz, funk, and world rhythms to create a distinct sonic landscape for the band. Offstage, Roger is an avid cook, often experimenting with Cajun and Creole cuisine, and he's never without his collection of antique percussion instruments, which he's been collecting since childhood.
